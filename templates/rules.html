Foosball Rules

Objective

Score more goals than your opponent within the time limit or until a set number of goals is reached.

Setup

  • The table is divided into two halves by a central barrier.
  • Each player/team controls rods with players, aiming to score in the opponent's goal.
  • Standard tables have 8 rods (3-man, 2-man, 5-man, goalie) for each side.

Gameplay

  • Coin toss decides who serves first; after a goal, the scored-on team serves.
  • Only the rods closest to the goal can be used to put the ball into play from a dead ball situation.
  • Players must only touch their assigned rods and cannot interfere with the ball directly.
  • The ball must touch two men on the 5-bar before being passed forward.
  • A spinning rod (360-degree rotation) without touching the ball is a foul.
  • Time limits apply to ball possession (usually 15 seconds for defense/goalie, 10 seconds for midfield).

Scoring

A goal is scored when the ball fully passes the goal line.

Games are often played to 5 or 10 goals, or with a set time limit.

Fouls and Penalties

  • Touching the ball, jarring the table, or unsportsmanlike conduct are fouls.
  • Penalties can include loss of possession, a free kick for the opponent, or even a goal awarded.
  • Jarring the table results in the opposing team being awarded the ball on the 5-bar rod.
  • A ball that is stuck is awarded to the defensive team.

Special Rules (Optional)

  • "No lift" rule: Prohibits lifting the ball off the table except during service.
  • "Three-strikes" rule: Disqualifies players after three warnings for misconduct.
